Welcome

My name is Roman Gonzalez, I'm a Software Developer who lives in the beautiful (and rainy) city of Vancouver BC. My goal is to produce simple usable quality products that fits into my clients needs, using Open Web Standards and Open Source tools that costs zip.

Because the Web is a big jungle, we will develop the applications to support the long variety of browsers used out there (at least the popular ones), squeezing out the best from the new ones, and making it work nice and dandy on the old ones.

We are advocate on the use of agile practices for software development, so feedback and code quality is a main concern to us, code quality left behind will be a no-no. With the use of agile practices, you will be more on control in the development of the project, and at the same time, you will have a more understandable and reliable code base that can be easily taken by other developers that might join in the future.

The gory details...

So you want to know more about me? Well, I was born in September 21th 1985, on Caracas, Venezuela. I've played with computers since I was kid, with my oldest brother on our first XT Turbo (8Mhz, 20 MB of RAM); from then on, I always used the computer to play on-line games and check the e-mail or chatting.

After I graduated from High-school, I started to study the Computer Science branch on Universidad Central de Venezuela (Central University of Venezuela) starting in 2002. From there I began to get passionate about algorithms, operative systems and high level problems. At some point in my career (2004) I started a half-time course with IBM called "Training for development of e-Business applications".

After finishing the course with IBM, I started my professional career working at some places inside the university, later on, I started to work in Terabrain Communications, and after that in has_many :developers developing application for clients world-wide.

At the same time, I was participating in the ACM ICPC with my very talented team Guayoyo representing our University. We competed against all the universities of the North Latin American region (Colombia, Venezuela), obtaining the firsts spots that enabled us to compete on the world finals competitions for two years in a row.

After graduating in July 2008, I moved to one of the prettiest cities I've ever seen, Vancouver BC. Since then, I started a new career as freelance developer and Open Source developer.

We are not cooking with fire anymore...

As you may know, the Web has a lot of options for tools of development, some of them are free, some others are not that free. In our case, we will be working with high quality Open Source technologies, highly used in the Web today.

Starting with the Client based technologies, I can work with the following:

  • Javascript: jQuery, Mootools, Prototype
  • CSS: Blueprint

On the server side, I can work with the following:

  • Ruby: Rails, Merb
  • Python: Django, Google App Engine
  • Servers: Apache, Ngnix
  • Databases: PostgreSQL, MySQL, CouchDB
  • Cache: Memcached

Besides that, we can make some protocols work for you, these might be helpful for making your app more popular, like OpenID or Facebook Connect.

In case you are wondering, this page was made with a dash of Mootools, and a large dose of Google App Web Engine ;-)
